Mohanlal Viswanathan Nair (born 21 May 1960), better known by his stage name Mohanlal, is an Indian actor, producer, singer, and writer who is best known for his work in Malayalam films. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest living actors in Indian cinema, and is renowned for his versatile and natural acting style.Despite the fact that Mohanlal had his acting debut in Thiranottam (1978), the film was not released at the time owing to censorship concerns; the picture was only released 25 years later in five cinemas. After that, he landed a part as the antagonist in his first feature film, Manjil Virinja Pookkal (1980), when he was only 20 years old. With the economic success of Rajavinte Makan, he established himself as a well-known leading man (1986). Mohanlal also earned his first Kerala State Film Award for Best Actor in 1986 for his portrayal in T. P. Balagopalan M.A., which was directed by T. P. Balagopalan. Mohanlal has appeared in more than 300 Malayalam films throughout the course of his three-decade-long career, which has included roles in everything from comedic to action and romantic flicks. Mohanlal has also appeared in films from the Bollywood, Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada film industries. His most memorable performances were in Mani Ratnam’s Iruvar (1997) and Ram Gopal Varma’s Company (2002), both of which were directed by Mani Ratnam. Mohanlal is widely recognised as one of the best performers working in Indian film today, and he is also considered a cultural icon. As a matinee idol in Kerala’s popular culture, he is also known as the “Kerala Superstar.” In a 2006 online survey held by CNN-IBN on the occasion of the 50th anniversary of Kerala’s creation, he was voted the most popular Keralite in the state. In recognition of his achievements to Indian film, the Government of India awarded him the Padma Shri, India’s fourth highest civilian honour, in 200As a producer for Vanaprastham, he received four Indian National Film Awards: two for Best Actor (for his roles in Bharatham and Vanaprastham), one for Special Jury (for his role in Kireedam), and one for Best Film. He has also received six Kerala State Film Awards for Best Actor and eight Filmfare Awards for Best Actor. After being awarded the honorary title of Lieutenant Colonel in the Territorial Army of India in 2009, he went on to get an honorary doctorate from the Sree Sankaracharya University of Sanskrit in Kerala, making him the first and only actor to receive such a distinction. In 2012, he became the first and only South Indian actor to be awarded the status of Black belt in Taekwondo by the South Korean government, which was bestowed on him by the World Taekwondo Headquarters in Korea.
