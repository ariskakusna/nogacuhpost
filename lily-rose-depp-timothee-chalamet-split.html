Lily-Rose Depp and Timothe Chalamet Have Reportedly Split

Lily-Rose Depp and Timothée Chalamet have apparently called it quits on their year-long romance.

As reported by People, Timothée is featured in British Vogue's May 2020 issue, in which he notes he's "currently single." The pair's alleged split was also confirmed by sources at Us Weekly.

The young actors met on the set of Netflix's historical drama The King, where they portray a married couple, with Chalamet taking on the role of Henry V, and Depp as his wife, Catherine. By October 2018, they were spotted together embracing off-screen in New York City.

While Lily-Rose and Timothée never explicitly confirmed the status of their relationship, they subtly let the world know they were together at last year's Venice Film Festival when they walked the red carpet as a couple. Just days later, they were photographed making out aboard a boat in Capri, Italy.

Back in October, Depp gushed about her co-star during an interview with Entertainment Tonight, calling Chalamet "incredible" and "so, so great" at his job.

“It's always exciting to work with somebody who you know has given themselves so wholeheartedly to their role and is so invested,” she said at the time. “It can be nerve-wracking to work with people whose talent you admire so much, but hopefully it can only make things better.”

At this time, neither Lily-Rose nor Timothée have commented on their alleged breakup.
