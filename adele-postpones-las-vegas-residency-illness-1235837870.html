Adele Postpones Las Vegas Residency Due to Illness

Adele has revealed that she had to postpone several dates of her Las Vegas residency due to an illness that has taken a toll on her voice.

The Grammy-winning singer shared the news on her Instagram Tuesday, writing, “Sadly I have to take a beat and pause my Vegas residency. I was sick at the end of the last leg and all the way through my break,” she continued. “I hadn’t quite gotten the chance to get back to full health before shows resumed and now I’m sick again, and unfortunately it’s all taken a toll on my voice. And so on Doctors orders I have no choice but to rest thoroughly.”

The impacted shows include all her dates throughout March. “The remaining 5 weekends of this leg are being postponed to a later date,” she added. “We are already working out the details and you will be sent the information asap.”

Adele also wrote in the post’s caption, “I love you, I’ll miss you like mad and I’m sorry for the inconvenience.”

In October, the “Easy on Me” singer announced that she was extending her Weekends with Adele residency at The Colosseum at Caesars Palace from January through June 2024.

The residency was initially supposed to kick off in January 2022, but the start got postponed due to production delays and team members battling COVID-19 at the time. Her residency eventually got underway in November 2022 and ran through March 2023 before Adele revealed another leg, which took place from June to November 2023. She then added 32 new dates in 2024.
