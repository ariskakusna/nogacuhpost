The Tradition That Started At Diana's Funeral And Continued With The Queen

published on August 10, 2024
The queen's coffin passed through the London Borough of Hounslow on its way to Windsor, and the borough prepared by advising onlookers on how to behave. Specifically, the borough's officials asked that if onlookers wanted to throw flowers, they only throw one each, with any wrapping or packaging removed. They also asked that the crowd not throw anything other than flowers, including teddy bears and full bouquets or flower arrangements.
