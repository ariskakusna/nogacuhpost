Marc Lore’s Early Life and Education

Marc Lore was born and raised in Staten Island, New York. He graduated from Bucknell University with a degree in Business Management. His entrepreneurial spirit was evident from a young age, and he started his first business while still in college.

Entrepreneurial Journey

Lore’s entrepreneurial journey began with the creation of The Pit, an Internet sports stock market, which he later sold to Topps Company. He then co-founded Quidsi, the parent company of Diapers.com and Soap.com, which was sold to Amazon for $545 million in 2010. His most notable venture, Jet.com, was sold to Walmart for $3.3 billion in 2016, marking one of the largest e-commerce deals in U.S. history.

Role at Walmart

Following the acquisition of Jet.com, Lore became the CEO of Walmart eCommerce U.S., where he spearheaded the company’s online growth strategy. Under his leadership, Walmart’s online sales grew significantly, strengthening the company’s position in the e-commerce market.

Post-Walmart Ventures

In January 2021, Lore announced his departure from Walmart to focus on his entrepreneurial ventures. He has since launched a venture capital firm, Wonder, and is reportedly working on a project to build a city of the future, known as Telosa.

Real Estate Investments

Lore’s wealth is also tied to his real estate investments. He owns several properties in New Jersey and Florida, including a $43.8 million penthouse in Manhattan.

Philanthropy
