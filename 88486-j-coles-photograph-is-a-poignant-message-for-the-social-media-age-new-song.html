J. Cole's &quot;Photograph&quot; Is A Poignant Message For The Social Media Age

KOD arrived at midnight, and has since been followed by a dope music video for the stand-out cut "ATM." That's not the only stand-out though, with a tracklist of just twelve tracks, he barely missed a step too (meaning: whole album is stand-out material). Of course, the "1985" album outro is shaking things up in the e-streets, and we also gave the title track an extra look before asking you guys what song we should highlight next. It was definitely a toss-up between "Motiv8" and "Photographs" but we're going with the latter (we added all four of these songs to our Fire Emoji playlist though).

"Photograph" is a smooth joint, and yet another example of J. Cole's smooth, well-oiled flow. The twang of a guitar and shuttering hi-hats are backing Cole as he tells a familiar tale of social media pursuits -- the hook should be especially poignant with the millennial/internet-bred age.
