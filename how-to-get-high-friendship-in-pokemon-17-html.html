How To Get A High Friendship In Pokemon Violet

People are currently reading this guide.

Poké-BFFs in Pokémon Violet: From Zero to Hero (Friendship-wise, that is)

Let's face it, trainers. We all want that squad of Pokémon who adore us more than leftover poffins (and let's be real, those are pretty darn delicious). But in Pokémon Violet, building that unshakeable trainer-Pokémon bond can feel trickier than a Ditto impersonating a Wobbuffet. Fear not, fellow monster tamers! This guide will turn you into a friendship guru and have your Pokémon showering you with affection (or at least, not trying to trip you with Vine Whip).

Step 1: Ditch the Pokéball, Not the Party

  • Party Time, All the Time: Forget those fancy Poké Balls – just keep your Pokémon buddy in your active party. Every step you take together strengthens the bond. Think of it as a long, scenic walk with your best friend... except your best friend might occasionally breathe fire at a passing Budew.

Step 2: Level Up Your Lovin'

  • Battle Buddies: Battles are a classic way to build friendship. Every victory (and even some defeats) bring you and your Pokémon closer. Just be a responsible trainer and keep them healthy! A fainted Pokémon is not a happy Pokémon.

Step 3: Embrace the Power of Picnics

  • Picnic Perfect: Pokémon Violet introduces the glorious world of picnics! These aren't just for catching a glimpse of your Professor's questionable sandwich-making skills (although, that is a delightful bonus). Playing with your Pokémon, feeding them yummy curries (though maybe avoid the charcoal-grilled ones), and simply hanging out all boost that friendship level.

Step 4: Accessorize for Success (and Cuteness)

  • The Soothe Bell: This handy held item is a friendship amplifier, plain and simple. Slap it on your Pokémon and watch the friendship points soar! Just don't be surprised if they start asking you to borrow it for their next hot date.

Step 5: Leveled-Up Luxury

  • Luxury Ball Bonus: Feeling fancy? Catch your Pokémon with a Luxury Ball. These snazzy Poké Balls come with a built-in friendship boost, giving your new catch a head start on the friendship train.

Bonus Tip: Ditch the Bitter

  • Herbal Medicine Mishap: Avoid using Bitter Herbs on your Pokémon! These might heal status conditions, but they also come with a nasty side effect – a dip in friendship. So, unless your Pokémon is secretly plotting a coup, stick to Potions and Revives.

Remember, Trainers: Friendship is a Two-Way Street

Building a strong bond with your Pokémon is about more than just grinding friendship points. Listen to their cries (even the earsplitting ones), respect their battle preferences (fire types probably don't appreciate being sent out against water gyms), and shower them with affection (both virtual and, if your wallet allows, real-life plushie form). With a little time and effort, you'll have a team of Pokémon who'd walk through fire for you (with proper type matchups, of course). Now get out there and explore the vast world of Pokémon Violet with your best pals by your side!
